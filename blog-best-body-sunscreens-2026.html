How Much to Apply

Most people apply only 25-50% of the recommended amount, which can reduce SPF 50 protection to SPF 12 or less.

  • Full body: 1 ounce (a shot glass full) for a full-body application in a swimsuit
  • Each arm: One finger-length strip from the tube
  • Each leg: Two finger-length strips
  • Torso front and back: Two finger-length strips each
  • Reapply every 2 hours — and immediately after swimming, sweating, or toweling off
  • A 6oz bottle should last: 3-4 full body applications. If a bottle lasts you all summer, you are not using enough

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I reapply sunscreen?

Every 2 hours when outdoors, or immediately after swimming, sweating, or toweling off. Set a phone timer to remind yourself.

Is SPF 30 or SPF 50 better?

SPF 30 blocks 97% of UVB rays. SPF 50 blocks 98%. The difference seems small, but SPF 50 provides a critical safety margin because most people under-apply. SPF 50 is recommended for extended outdoor exposure.

Does sunscreen expire?

Yes. Most sunscreens expire after 3 years from manufacture date. Expired sunscreen may have reduced SPF protection. Check the expiration date printed on the bottle and replace annually.

Do I need sunscreen on cloudy days?

Yes. Up to 80% of UV rays penetrate clouds. You can get sunburned on overcast days. Apply sunscreen whenever you will be outdoors for more than 15 minutes, regardless of cloud cover.

Is mineral or chemical sunscreen better?

Neither is universally better. Chemical sunscreens are more cosmetically elegant with no white cast. Mineral sunscreens are better for sensitive skin and provide immediate protection. Choose based on your skin type and preferences.

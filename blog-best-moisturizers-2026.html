Best Face Moisturizers 2026: Dermatologist-Recommended

Chicago's climate is one of the harshest for skin in America — brutal dry winters with indoor heating that drops humidity to 15%, followed by humid summers with UV exposure off Lake Michigan. Your moisturizer needs to handle both extremes. These picks are dermatologist-recommended and tested through Chicago's full weather cycle.

Best Overall: CeraVe Moisturizing Cream ($17/16oz)

The gold standard recommended by more dermatologists than any other brand. Contains three essential ceramides (1, 3, 6-II) that restore the skin barrier — critical after Chicago winter destroys it. Hyaluronic acid draws moisture into skin. MVE technology releases ingredients over 24 hours. Fragrance-free, non-comedogenic. Available literally everywhere in Chicago — Walgreens, CVS, Target, Costco. The 16oz jar with pump is the best value in skincare.

Best for Dry Skin: La Roche-Posay Cicaplast Baume B5+ ($18)

Intense repair balm for skin that's cracked, chapped, or windburned. Madecassoside (from centella asiatica) soothes irritation. Panthenol (vitamin B5) accelerates skin repair. This is the product Chicago dermatologists recommend for the February face — that dry, red, flaky skin from walking to the L in -10°F wind chill. Apply at night as the last step over your regular moisturizer.

Best for Oily Skin: Neutrogena Hydro Boost Gel ($20)

Water-based gel formula hydrates without adding oil. Hyaluronic acid holds 1,000x its weight in water. Oil-free, non-comedogenic, won't clog pores. Absorbs in seconds, works perfectly under makeup and SPF. The best option for Chicago summers when humidity and sweat make heavy creams feel suffocating.

Chicago Winter Skincare Routine

  1. Gentle cleanser (CeraVe Hydrating, $15) — no foaming cleansers in winter, they strip oils
  2. Hyaluronic acid serum (The Ordinary, $8) — apply to damp skin only. In Chicago's dry air, HA can pull moisture FROM skin if applied to dry skin
  3. Moisturizer — CeraVe Cream or richer formula than you use in summer
  4. Occlusive layer at night — Aquaphor or CeraVe Healing Ointment over moisturizer. Seals everything in
  5. SPF in morning — yes, even in winter. UV reflects off snow and penetrates clouds. EltaMD UV Clear ($39) is the dermatologist favorite

Humidifier: The Secret Weapon

Every Chicago dermatologist will tell you: get a humidifier. Indoor heating drops humidity to 15-20% (healthy skin needs 40-60%). A humidifier in your bedroom prevents overnight moisture loss. The Levoit LV600S ($90) covers 750 sq ft and runs 60 hours on a single fill. Your skin, sinuses, and sleep quality will all improve.

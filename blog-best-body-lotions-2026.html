Best for Dry Skin

CeraVe Moisturizing Cream ($17 for 19oz) — The dermatologist gold standard. Contains three essential ceramides (1, 3, 6-II) that repair and maintain the skin barrier. Hyaluronic acid draws moisture into the skin. MVE technology releases ingredients slowly for 24-hour hydration. Fragrance-free, non-comedogenic, and suitable for eczema-prone skin. If you buy one body moisturizer, make it this one.

Eucerin Original Healing Cream ($12 for 16oz) — For severely dry, cracked skin. Rich, occlusive formula seals in moisture. Contains glycerin and lanolin for intense hydration. Particularly effective for winter dryness, cracked hands, and rough elbows. Fragrance-free. Too heavy for summer use on most people but perfect for extreme dryness.

Aveeno Daily Moisturizing Lotion ($10 for 18oz) — Best daily lotion for dry skin. Colloidal oatmeal soothes and protects. Non-greasy formula absorbs within a minute. Clinically proven to improve dry skin in one day. Gentle enough for sensitive skin. The best value in body moisturizers.

Best for Sensitive Skin

Vanicream Moisturizing Cream ($14 for 16oz) — Formulated without dyes, fragrance, lanolin, parabens, or formaldehyde. Recommended by dermatologists for patients with eczema, psoriasis, and contact dermatitis. Simple ingredient list minimizes reaction risk. The jar version is richer than the pump version.

La Roche-Posay Lipikar Balm AP+M ($20 for 13.5oz) — Contains shea butter and niacinamide to restore the skin barrier. Prebiotic thermal water supports healthy skin microbiome. Triple-action formula: repairs, soothes, and protects. Suitable for babies, children, and adults with very sensitive or eczema-prone skin.

Best Lightweight Lotions

Lubriderm Daily Moisture ($8 for 16oz) — Lightweight, fast-absorbing, and non-greasy. Contains vitamin B5 for skin conditioning. Perfect for normal-to-slightly-dry skin. Absorbs in under 30 seconds so you can get dressed immediately. Best budget lightweight option.

Neutrogena Hydro Boost Body Gel Cream ($10 for 16oz) — Hyaluronic acid in a gel-cream texture that feels like water on the skin. Zero residue, zero greasiness. Ideal for oily skin types or hot weather. Provides genuine hydration without any heaviness. The best body moisturizer for people who hate body moisturizer.

Best Anti-Aging Body Lotions

AmLactin Daily Moisturizing Lotion ($14 for 14.1oz) — Contains 12% lactic acid that gently exfoliates while hydrating. Smooths rough, bumpy skin (keratosis pilaris) on arms, thighs, and legs. Clinically proven to improve skin texture and radiance. The pH-balanced formula does not sting or irritate. Note: slight tangy smell that fades quickly.

Gold Bond Age Renew Crepe Corrector ($12 for 8oz) — Targets crepey skin on arms, legs, and neck. Contains peptides and AHAs to firm and smooth. Results visible within 2-4 weeks of daily use. Affordable anti-aging body care that actually shows results.

Application Tips

  • Apply to damp skin: Moisturizer locks in 30% more hydration when applied within 3 minutes of showering, while skin is still slightly damp
  • Use enough product: A full-body application requires about a golf ball-sized amount. Most people under-apply, reducing effectiveness
  • Focus on dry areas: Elbows, knees, heels, and shins need extra attention. Apply a second layer to these spots
  • Morning vs night: Lightweight lotions in the morning under clothes. Rich creams at night for overnight absorption
  • Don't forget SPF: Body lotion does not replace sunscreen. Apply SPF 30+ to exposed areas during the day

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best body lotion for very dry skin?

CeraVe Moisturizing Cream is the top dermatologist recommendation for very dry skin. Its ceramide-based formula repairs the skin barrier while providing 24-hour hydration.

Should I use lotion or cream?

Lotions are lighter and absorb faster, best for normal skin and warm weather. Creams are thicker and more hydrating, best for dry skin and cold weather. Ointments are the most occlusive for extremely dry or damaged skin.

How often should I moisturize my body?

Apply body moisturizer at least once daily, ideally after showering. In winter or in dry climates, twice daily application provides better results.

Can body lotion cause acne on the body?

Yes, heavy or comedogenic lotions can cause body acne (bacne). If you are prone to body breakouts, use non-comedogenic formulas like Neutrogena Hydro Boost or CeraVe Moisturizing Lotion (pump version, not tub).

Is expensive body lotion worth it?

For most people, no. CeraVe, Aveeno, and Vanicream all cost under $20 and are recommended by dermatologists over many luxury brands. The active ingredients in affordable lotions are identical to premium options.
